Cosmic Void Meditation

  1. Posture
    Sit erect, legs crossed—on the floor, grass, cushion, or stone—in a quiet place. If you prefer, lie down, taking care not to fall asleep. Close your eyes or fix your gaze softly on the Celestial Triad Spiral.
  2. Breath Awareness
    Inhale slowly through the nose, letting your belly rise and fall (not your chest). Count in to five, pause to four, then exhale to three. Adjust counts to what feels natural.
  3. Release Tension
    With each cycle, notice areas of tightness—jaw, shoulders, belly—and soften them one by one. Continue this gentle unwinding for about five minutes.
  4. Sonorous Presence
    With eyes closed, widen your attention to ambient sounds. Hear each arise from silence and return to it—no labels, no judgments—just pure listening.
  5. The Primordial Void
    Imagine yourself as the lone light in infinite blackness—first spark in the void, dreaming the cosmos into being. You are the universe’s original inspiration.
  6. Resting in Creation
    Remain here in timeless stillness. Observe every breath, every thought, every sound as part of the great unfolding. There is nowhere else to go.
  7. Return Gently
    When ready, let ordinary thoughts drift back like gentle waves on an alpine lake. Open your eyes softly. You're back.

Practice daily, even if only for a few minutes. Meditation is the easiest way to reconnect with the self.

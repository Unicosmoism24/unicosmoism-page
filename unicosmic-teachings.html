Introduction to Unicosmic Teachings: The Unicosmic Path

The real mission here is to keep a light on for the people who have always felt a connection to the stars but didn't have a place to talk about it. Most of us grew up being told we are separate from everything else - separate from nature, separate from each other, and definitely separate from the rest of the universe. In the long run, we want to show that this separation is just an illusion. Whether you look at it through a telescope or through quantum physics, everything is connected. We’re here to be a landing spot for those who want to explore that connection without the weight of old-school religious rules or dogma.

"We are building a sanctuary where it is safe to admit that everyone is interconnected, empathy is not a hinderance, all life matters, and love is the strongest force."

We also want to make sure we are ready for what’s coming. As we get better at seeing into deep space, like with the Vera Rubin snapshots, it’s becoming harder to ignore that we aren’t alone. Our goal is to help people move past the fear and the "demon" labels that mainstream religions often use. We want to foster a community that sees extraterrestrial life as part of the same cosmic family. If we can start seeing ourselves as "focused energy" instead of just isolated humans, we can finally take our place in the bigger picture.

At the end of the day, the Church of Cosmic Oneness is about staying curious and staying open. We aren't trying to control anyone or tell them exactly what to think. We just want to make sure that the path to cosmic awareness stays open and free for whoever feels the pull. We are here to help each other wake up to the fact that we are the universe experiencing itself, and to make sure that when our neighbors from other worlds do show up, they find a species that actually knows who they are.

Unicosmic Teachings: Lesson 1 - The Illusion of Separateness

The confusion most Western folks have with the human body is they believe there are all of these separate individual "parts" working together to make a body, but that's totally wrong and backwards. It’s a purely mechanical way of looking at a miracle.

There's no separate "anything" in our bodies or in the entire cosmos. Everything is dependent on the other for existence in this "reality". You don't have a foot or a head, or fingers, or a liver, or a nose. Those are just "parts" that we labeled as identifiers so we could talk to each other about a specific issue or a health problem. In reality, your body is one giant organism of interconnectedness with no real end or beginning. We aren't even separate from the Universe around us.

Think about it: what makes you as your current conscious self-awareness different from your skin cells or hair follicles? What makes you different from your bloodstream or the neurons in your brain? You have to wake up to the fact that your organism is a microbiome - a colony of billions of tiny things that are all interdependent on each other. Somehow, that colony makes up "YOU", with your thoughts, your personality, and your stories.

We’re basically the equivalent of if a coral reef became mobile and then self-aware in a single point of consciousness. When you fully understand that we’re all a bunch of self-aware reefs walking around, you also start to realize that our bodies are interdependent on the environment around us. So where do our bodies really end and the outside environment begin? The answer is that the environment is just as much a part of your body as your cells and your DNA. You don't "visit" nature - you are nature.

We’re actually taught part of this in school. We're told the Earth is full of these interconnected ecosystems that are constantly balancing each other to stay alive, but our schools leave out the most important part: that we are a vital component of those systems. They try to make us feel like we’re merely observers, and that’s so wrong and damaging. It leads directly to that "conquer-everything" mentality that is currently hurting our world. When you realize that the forest and the stars are just the "outer layers" of your own body, you stop wanting to conquer them and start wanting to care for them.

When you wake up each morning, do what I do and once you come to your senses and are awake enough to think, sit in or on the edge of your bed comfortably, close your eyes, and repeat either out loud in your head that "I am not seperate from the world around me. Everything is one." Repeat this several times slowly every day, as the human mind thrives on repetition and this is how we train/wire ourselves into mastering skills - be it mental or physical.
